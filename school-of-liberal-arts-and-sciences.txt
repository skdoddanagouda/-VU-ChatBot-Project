Learning Beyond Lectures
At the School of Liberal Arts and Sciences, learning extends beyond the classroom, immersing students in a rich array of experiences that deepen analytical skills, ignite intellectual curiosity, and inspire impactful action on a global scale
Community Engagement
Community Engagement
Psychology students visited AMBA, gaining profound insights from a community supporting individuals with intellectual disabilities, demonstrating our commitment to community engagement and the critical analysis of social impact, ethical responsibilities, and humanistic values.
Experiential Learning & Global Engagement
Experiential Learning & Global Engagement
We offer a rich array of experiential learning opportunities, including the transformative India Immersion Programme and extensive internships, allowing students to critically examine diverse cultures, societal structures, and policy challenges through direct, immersive engagement.
Vibrant Intellectual & Creative Pursuits
Vibrant Intellectual & Creative Pursuits
Students can also participate in various university-wide clubs and engaging performing arts electives, fostering well-rounded development and the pursuit of intellectual and creative passions through active dialogue, rigorous debate, and artistic expression within a vibrant academic community.
Student Career Development
Student Career Development
Student career development is supported by extensive internships, providing practical experience, and preparing graduates for diverse and influential pathways in research, policy, public service, international affairs, academia, business, and beyond.

Research, Re-thinking and Creative Impact
Research at our School is fundamentally driven by a commitment to addressing societal needs and advancing human understanding, contributing original, authoritative insights to global academic and public discourse.  Our student achievements highlight their formidable capacity for impactful research and advanced critical thinking: Our Psychology students have presented significant research on neurodiversity and virtual therapies at national conferences, demonstrating their ability to critically analyze complex phenomenas, synthesize scholarly literature, and propose innovative, ethically-sound solutions. Economics students have engaged in impactful projects connecting academic learning with real-world issues like healthcare economics and political economy, translating theoretical frameworks into profound practical insights.

Faculty Thought Leadership
Our faculty are not only exceptional educators but also pioneering intellectuals who shape national and global discourse. Their work is cited in key policy platforms, scholarly journals, and public debates, reinforcing the School's commitment to intellectual excellence and relevance. Their work exemplifies the School's ethos—scholarship that matters, rooted in critical inquiry, and designed to make a difference.
