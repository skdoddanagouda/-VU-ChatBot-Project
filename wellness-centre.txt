EXPERIENCE A  HOLISTIC APPROACH TO
WELLBEING
Welcome to the Wellness Centre at Vidyashilp
University! Our mission is to cultivate a
supportive environment dedicated to enhancing
mental health and overall wellbeing. We are
committed to providing a holistic university
experience for both students and staff, ensuring
that everyone has access to essential resources
and programs that empower them to thrive.
SERVICES OFFERED
The Wellness Centre at Vidyashilp University provides psychological
support for students and staff. A qualified and experienced counselling
psychologist will provide services in a completely confidential setting.
These may include:
Understanding the difficulties they are experiencing
Devising an action plan to address their concerns.
Enhancing their self-awareness.
Helping them develop resilience and enable them to achieve their
academic and personal goals.
We are dedicated to offering personalized support to help you navigate
your mental health journey effectively. The counsellor will address issues
such as stress, depression, anxiety, academic difficulties, family and
relationship problems, body image concerns, bullying, gender/sexual
identity concerns, self-harm, suicidal thoughts, substance abuse, and so
on.

EVENTS &
WORKSHOPS
At the Wellness Centre, we host a diverse range of engaging
events designed to educate our community about mental health
and empower individuals to lead balanced, fulfilling lives. Our
carefully crafted programs promote awareness, reduce stigma,
and equip participants with the essential tools needed to
manage mental health in their daily lives.
Join us for an array of workshops, seminars, competitions and
awareness campaigns focused on fostering a culture that
prioritizes well-being. We also collaborate with other student
clubs and organizations* to enhance mental health and
promote wellness.

Inclusive Education at the Higher Education Workshop
X
An immersive workshop was conducted, by Dr. Alka Patidar, focusing on the instructional
challenges faced by students in higher education spaces and their impact on performance.
The influence of a policy-driven approach and a belonging-focused approach were
emphasized along with the difference it makes for the student.
In-depth discussions on medical and social models of disability, both visible and invisible,
were held. Key challenges in, and accommodations for, different neurodivergent conditions
were understood.
Resources on assessing and accommodating students with diverse needs were shared.
Common misconceptions were dismantled, and action plans for reflecting acceptance and
support at a policy level were provided.
Psychological First Responders Training
X
Led by Dr. Gargi Kumar and Dr. Sheetal Lakhani, the training session provided faculty and
staff with guidance on distinguishing between crisis and non-crisis situations that may arise
in educational settings. The training included instruction on immediate response
procedures, development of skills in empathetic listening and authentic curiosity, and
strategies for self-care for responders.
Dr. Kumar facilitated discussions on Psychological First Aid (PFA), focusing on assessment
protocols, proper documentation practices, professionalism, and establishing appropriate
boundaries in responder-student interactions. The training also covered effective
documentation strategies and follow-up procedures for PFA cases, highlighting the
importance of confidentiality throughout the process.
Wellness Centre Orientation – Stress and Mental Health Services
X
Presented by Dr. Girishchandra, Psychiatrist at Maarga Psychiatric Hospital (VU’s Official
Mental Health Partner), and Mr. Vinayak Pai. During the session, they discussed the nature
of stress—its positive and negative implications—as well as its causes, effects, and effective
management strategies, including time management and internal coping mechanisms. The
presentation included an interactive Q&A with the student body of VU, focusing on
maintaining mental health during exams and managing external stressors, complemented
by sharing personal experiences and coping strategies.
Dr. Girishchandra elaborated on the mental health services provided by Maarga Psychiatric
Hospital, which will be offer on-campus support twice weekly and emergency assistance
when needed. Contact information for the hospital was also shared.
World Suicide Prevention Day Campaign
X
The VU Wellness Centre, in collaboration with the Wellness, Photography, and Music Clubs,
hosted an event in the common area near the canteen to observe World Suicide Prevention
Day. The occasion featured performances by the Music Club, including songs promoting self-
love, as well as a dance presentation by the Dance Club centered around the theme ";Choose
Life."; Additionally, the Photography Club displayed a photo series titled";Changing the
Narrative on Suicide.";
Tanushree N, a member of the student body, led a discussion on the significance of suicide
prevention, early warning signs, and the services provided by the Wellness Centre. The
event was well-attended by students and staff, reflecting the campus community’s
dedication to mental health awareness and suicide prevention initiatives.
World Mental Health Day Celebration with Theatre Club
X
World Mental Health Day was commemorated with the inauguration of the Theatre Club –
";Play Your Part."; The event included discussions centered on this year's theme, “It’s Time to
Prioritize Mental Health in the Workplace,” emphasizing its significance for both students
and faculty.
The program featured student performances, a recital from a well-known play, and a
comedic presentation on the risks associated with self-diagnosis. Additionally, the campus
counselor was introduced to the student body, along with an overview of the various
services available through the Wellness Centre.
Faculty Mental Health Workshop
X
The workshop was facilitated by Dr. Gayathri and Ms. Akshita Kaushik of Maarga Psychiatric
Hospital. They dispelled myths about mental health in university contexts and explored
definitions and signs of poor mental health while participants shared their personal
experiences.
The facilitators discussed stress factors, including demands, resources, and personal
reactions, and participants identified various stressors they faced. They provided
information about biological responses to stress and discussed coping strategies, such as
reframing tasks and specific techniques for memory, concentration, and decision-making.
The group addressed work-life balance and setting realistic expectations in follow-up
discussions. The facilitators also encouraged discussion on the shared responsibilities
between employees and employers in fostering a psychologically safe environment, with
participants sharing scenarios to explore constructive approaches.
Psychological First Responders Workshop for Student Volunteers
X
Ms. Anusha GS from Maarga Psychiatric Hospital and Mr. Anikethana C H facilitated an
engaging workshop focused on enhancing mental health awareness among students and
equipping them with vital peer support skills. The session aimed to build crisis management
abilities and improve communication techniques to offer reassurance during mental health
emergencies. Participants explored key concepts such as the distinctions between general
health and mental health, the differences between panic and anxiety attacks, grounding
techniques, and suicide prevention strategies. Special emphasis was placed on recognizing
warning signs of mental health crises, practicing active listening, and maintaining safety and
dignity when supporting peers in sensitive situations.
The workshop also introduced Psychological First Aid, ethical decision-making, and
underscored the importance of self-care for peer supporters. Throughout the session,
students were encouraged to understand their personal limits to provide effective and
responsible support. This comprehensive training empowered students with the knowledge
and skills necessary to foster a compassionate and supportive campus community
Relationships and Sexual Mental Health Workshop
X
The session was led by Ms. Sanchia Aranha, who holds a Masters in Clinical Psychology from
MAHE and has completed comprehensive training in Sexuality Education with TARSHI. She
began by inviting participants to take part in an anonymous survey that explored
perceptions of modern dating Throughout the session, Ms. Sanchia guided participants
through the evolution of relationships, examining how societal and familial influences shape
our experiences, and the importance of self-worth independent of relationship status.
Topics also included coping strategies during breakups, the impact of social media, and the
value of self-reflection.
Ms. Sanchia, during the workshop, focused on understanding various types of
boundaries—including physical, emotional, digital, and sexual—and examined gender and
sexual identity through the concept of the Gender Bread Person, emphasizing the fluidity of
identities and the importance of individual definitions.
The session also addressed essential aspects of sexual mental health, including body image,
performance anxiety, consent, and coercion. Ms. Sanchia used an interactive quiz and group
discussion to debunk common myths about pregnancy, menstrual health, masturbation, and
sexually transmitted infections. She concluded by sharing resources available for sexual
health and hosting a Q&A session, where participants could seek further clarification and
support.

Frequently Asked Questions ?
How do I schedule an appointment with
a counsellor or therapist?
To schedule an appointment with a counsellor you can fill an online form. Alternatively, if it's
urgent please feel free to walk in to meet the counsellor directly or reach out to the
coordinators.We have a Psychologist on campus to provide face to face counselling
services. In addition, if you want to consult outside VU Wellness Centre, feel free to access our
list of additional resources.
Are mental health services confidential?
Yes, as mentioned in the previous page, confidentiality is a top priority in our mental health
services. Information shared with counsellors, therapists, or mental health professionals is
kept strictly confidential, except in cases where there is a risk of harm to oneself or others,
or as required by law.
What should I expect during a
counselling or therapy session?
Counselling or therapy sessions typically involve discussing your concerns, feelings,
and experiences in a supportive and nonjudgmental environment. Your therapist will
work with you to explore issues, develop coping strategies, and set goals for your
mental health and well-being.
Is there a limit to the number
of counselling sessions I can attend?
The number of counselling sessions may vary depending on individual needs and
circumstances. Some students may benefit from short-term counselling, while others
may require ongoing support. Your counsellor will work with you to determine the most
appropriate treatment plan for your situation.
What should I do in case of a mental health emergency?
If you are experiencing a mental health crisis or emergency, please contact our
Wellness Centre immediately or seek assistance from a group of faculty, staff and student
volunteers of the Wellness Centre. We have protocols in place to
provide prompt support and assistance in such situations.
Are there resources available for managing
stress and improving mental well-being?
Absolutely! We provide resources and workshops on stress management, mindfulness,
relaxation techniques, time management, and other skills to promote mental well-being
and resilience. Additionally, our Wellness Centre offers self-help materials, books and
online resources for students to access at any time.
How can I support a friend or peer who
may be struggling with their mental health?
If you are concerned about a friend
or peer, you can encourage them to
seek help at our Wellness Centre or
accompany them to an
appointment. Listen
non-judgmentally, offer support and
empathy, and encourage them to
take care of themselves. You can
also refer them to resources
available on campus.
