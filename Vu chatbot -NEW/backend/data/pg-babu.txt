About Prof. P.G. Babu
Biography
A highly experienced academic and administrator, Prof. P.G. Babu served for over
three decades as a Professor at the Indira Gandhi Institute of Development
Research (IGIDR), Mumbai, an institution established by the Reserve Bank of India,
where he was also the Dean of Graduate Studies. Prior to his current role at
Vidyashilp University, he was the Director of the Madras Institute of Development
Studies (MIDS) from 2019 to 2022.
His research and teaching interests are deeply interdisciplinary, focusing on
Microeconomic theory and Game theory with applications across fields such as
Environmental and Natural Resource Economics, Law and Economics, and Politics-
Philosophy and Economics. His significant research contributions include work on
the decision-making framework for climate change as part of the IPCC working
group, which earned him a citation from the Prime Minister of India and was
associated with the Nobel Memorial Peace Prize in 2007.
Globally recognized, Professor Babu held the prestigious Adam Smith Chair
Professorship in Philosophy and Economics at the University of Bayreuth, Germany,
for multiple summer semesters. He has been the Mumbai Coordinator for the
Erasmus Plus European Masters and Doctoral Programs in Law and Economics
since 2009. Additionally, he has held prominent editorial positions, currently serving
as the Managing Editor of the Journal of Quantitative Economics (Springer Nature)
and previously as the Editor-in-Chief of the Review of Development and
Change (Sage). An alumnus of the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), where he
earned his Ph.D., Professor Babu is also a member of the Board of Governors of the
Institute of Economic Growth (IEG) Delhi.
