Biography
Anchored in the deep waters of legal academia and advocacy, Mr. Anurag Sarthi is a law professor,
researcher, and writer whose work bridges the realms of maritime security, environmental
sustainability, and public law. He holds an LL.M. in Coastal and Maritime Security Law and
Governance from Rashtriya Raksha University (Distinction, 2nd Rank) and is currently pursuing a
Ph.D. in Law at the University of Delhi. He has also earned a Post Graduate Diploma in Environmental
Law and Policy from NLU Delhi with a Grade A (First Class).
Mr. Sarthi brings a unique blend of academic rigor and practical insight, having served as an advocate
enrolled with the Bar Council of Delhi and as a faculty member at Raffles Law School, Raffles
University. His teaching portfolio includes subjects such as Law of Torts, Environmental Law,
Administrative Law, Law of the Sea, and Alternative Dispute Resolution. During his tenure, he
actively contributed to institutional development through NAAC coordination, moot court initiatives, and
career counselling sessions.
A recognized researcher, he has published Scopus-indexed book chapters and articles on climate
change, maritime governance, and legal adaptation to emerging technologies. His recent editorial work
includes the book Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita: A Departure from the Colonial Era. He has also presented
papers at numerous national and international conferences, with accolades such as the Best Paper
Presenter Award validating his scholarly contributions.
Mr. Sarthi has served as a judge for several national moot court competitions and has contributed to
legal academia by drafting moot propositions for prominent events. He has completed multiple
certifications including those by the ICRC and IARC. Anurag's creative side shines through his interest
in street theatre, script writing, and innovative legal thinking—he is also the inventor of a Legal
Documentation Verification Kiosk, a design registered in 2024.
